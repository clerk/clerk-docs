Clerk provides multiple options for configuring authentication in your application. This section covers the sign-in methods available to your users, how to configure enterprise connections, and how to control who can sign up or modify their account.

You can modify your authentication options after your application has been created by navigating to the [Clerk Dashboard](https://dashboard.clerk.com/) and selecting any of the options under **User & authentication** in the left sidenav.

## Sign-in methods

Clerk supports a range of authentication methods:

- **Standard options** — email, phone, username, password, and passkeys. See [Sign-in options](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/sign-up-sign-in-options).
- **Multi-factor authentication (MFA)** — add a second verification step via SMS, authenticator app, or backup codes. See [Multi-factor authentication](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/multi-factor-authentication).
- **Social login** — let users sign in with their existing accounts from providers like Google, GitHub, Microsoft, and more. See [Social login](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/social-connections/overview).
- **Enterprise connections** — authenticate users via federated Identity Providers (e.g., Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace) using SAML, OIDC, or EASIE, with optional Directory Sync (SCIM). See [Enterprise connections](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/enterprise-connections/overview).
- **Web3 wallets** — let users sign in by proving ownership of a crypto wallet (e.g., MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet). See [Web3 wallets](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/web3/metamask).

## SSO connections

SSO connections are a way to allow users to authenticate with their existing accounts from other services.

Clerk offers two different types of SSO connections:

- **For all users** — also known as OAuth connections, or social connections. Read more about [social login](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/social-connections/overview).
- **For specific domains and Organizations** — also known as Enterprise SSO connections. Read more about [enterprise connections](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/enterprise-connections/overview).

To enable SSO connections, navigate to the [**SSO connections**](https://dashboard.clerk.com/~/user-authentication/sso-connections) page in the Clerk Dashboard.

## Web3 authentication

Clerk provides Web3 authentication with either [Base](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/web3/base), [MetaMask](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/web3/metamask), [Coinbase Wallet](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/web3/coinbase-wallet), or [OKX Wallet](/docs/guides/configure/auth-strategies/web3/okx-wallet). As part of validating the accuracy of the returned Web3 account address, Clerk handles the signing of a message and verifying the signature. Because sign-in with Web3 uses the same abstraction as our other authentication factors, like passwords or email links, other Clerk features like multi-factor authentication and profile enrichment work for Web3 users out-of-the-box.

To enable Web3 authentication, navigate to the [**Web3**](https://dashboard.clerk.com/~/user-authentication/web3) page in the Clerk Dashboard.

## Restrict changes

By default, users can add, remove, and modify their own email addresses, phone numbers, and usernames after sign-up. If you need tighter control over these identifiers, you can prevent users from changing them.

To restrict identifier changes:

1. In the Clerk Dashboard, navigate to the [**User & authentication**](https://dashboard.clerk.com/~/user-authentication/user-and-authentication) page.
1. Under the identifier you want to lock down (email address, phone number, or username), enable the **Restrict changes** setting.

When enabled:

- Users can still view their identifiers in [`<UserProfile />`](/docs/reference/components/user/user-profile) but cannot add, remove, or modify them.
- For email addresses, the restriction also prevents connecting an OAuth account that would add a new email address.
- Admins retain full control and can still modify user identifiers through the [Dashboard](https://dashboard.clerk.com/~/users) or the [Backend API](/docs/reference/backend-api).

> [!TIP]
> This feature prevents changes _after_ sign-up. If you want to control _who can sign up_ in the first place, see the restrictions section below.

## Restrictions

Clerk provides a set of restriction options designed to give you enhanced control over who can gain access to your application. Restrictions can limit sign-ups or prevent accounts with specific identifiers, such as email addresses, phone numbers, and even entire domains, from accessing your application. Learn more about [restrictions](/docs/guides/secure/restricting-access).
